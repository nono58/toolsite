Video Resolution FAQ

What is aspect ratio?

Aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between a video's width and height, expressed as W:H. 16:9 is the standard widescreen format used by YouTube and most modern displays. 9:16 (vertical) is used by TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. 1:1 is square, common for Instagram posts.

What is a safe zone in video?

Safe zones are inset areas of the frame where text and important graphics should stay to avoid being cropped by different screen sizes or overlaid by platform UI elements (like TikTok's caption area or YouTube's subscribe button). A typical safe zone is 90% of the frame width/height, centered.

What resolution should I export for YouTube?

YouTube recommends 1920×1080 (1080p) as the minimum for HD quality, and 3840×2160 (4K/UHD) for the highest quality. Always export at 16:9 aspect ratio for standard landscape videos. For Shorts, use 1080×1920 (9:16 vertical).

How do I calculate a new width from a height while keeping the ratio?

Width = Height × (original width / original height). For example, scaling 1920×1080 to a height of 720: Width = 720 × (1920/1080) = 1280. This tool does that calculation automatically — just enter the desired height and the width updates to match.
