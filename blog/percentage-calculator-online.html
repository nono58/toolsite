The Three Types of Percentage Problems

1. What is X% of Y?

Formula: Result = (X / 100) × Y

Example: What is 20% of 250? → (20/100) × 250 = 50

2. X is what percent of Y?

Formula: Percent = (X / Y) × 100

Example: 45 is what percent of 180? → (45/180) × 100 = 25%

3. Percentage Change

Formula: Change% = ((New - Old) / Old) × 100

Example: Price changed from $80 to $100 → ((100-80)/80) × 100 = +25%

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate 15% tip?
Multiply the bill by 0.15. For a $60 bill: 60 × 0.15 = $9 tip. Or use the calculator — enter 15% of 60.
What's the difference between percentage and percentage points?
If interest rates rise from 2% to 3%, that's a 1 percentage point increase, but a 50% increase in the rate. These are different things.
How do I reverse a percentage (find the original value)?
If the final value is $120 after a 20% increase: Original = 120 / 1.20 = $100.
How do I calculate percentage decrease?
Same as percentage change: ((New - Old) / Old) × 100. A negative result means a decrease.
